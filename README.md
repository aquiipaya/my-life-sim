## ğŸ”¬ Research Overview
[cite_start]This study challenges the traditional view of life as a mere dissipative structure[cite: 1, 10]. [cite_start]By using an agent-based model, we demonstrate that biological agents do not just accelerate entropy production but qualitatively transform how dissipation occurs[cite: 6, 12].

### Key Findings
* [cite_start]**Dissipation Rewiring**: Biological agents can suppress physical diffusive frictional heat ($heatDiff$) by up to 97%[cite: 7, 77].
* [cite_start]**Thermodynamic Regulation**: Life acts as a regulator that reorganizes dissipation channels to stabilize non-equilibrium environments[cite: 8, 127].
* [cite_start]**Phase Transition**: We identified a phase transition separating "suppression" and "acceleration" regimes based on inflow and consumption rates[cite: 100, 126].
